Fintech happenings around the world

UK

After the recent fundraising events, the Buy Now Pay Later pioneer, Klarna, has bagged a hefty valuation of $10.65B, becoming one of the world's top 5 highly valued private fintech firms. To stir a healthy competition in the European BNPL ecosystem, the Australian Fintech pinnacle, AfterPay, acquires Spanish firm Pagantis.

USA

CX is the next big game for Californian FinTechs like Robinhood. It is time for teens to get a grip on financial literacy, says Founder of Step, CJ MacDonald. From 60K to 4M Gen-Z and millennial users, Cleo has rapidly grown its user base, with more than 95% of customers prevailing in the USA.

CHINA

Alibaba's Ant is worth more than the gross domestic products of Chile, Egypt, or Finland. The new risk control mandates reiterate that the core finance remains the same for both banks and fintechs. As a result, China Banking Insurance and Regulation Commission (CBIRC) and its central bank pose a pause for the long-awaiting IPO of Ant Financial.

MENA

The central bank of the UAE, along with ADGM, has opened its new Fintech office and kicked off a wholesale central bank digital currency project called ABER. The initiative focuses on advanced cross-border payments and became popular partly because of the word’s origin. ‘Aber’ is an Arabic term which means wanderer, a perfect one to depict international money transfer. Finally, Amazon rebrands PAYFORT as Amazon payment services to start offering businesses competitive online payment processing.
